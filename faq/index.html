Questions we hear most often

What is Klypp and how is my media handled?

The Klypp mobile app moves your private/authorized media from your phone to your self-hosted media server. A relay operated by Klypp LLC simply routes the encrypted transfer; it never stores or inspects the payloads. The files remain on your devices and the server you control. For more privacy details, see the Privacy page.

Where do my files go during upload?

Uploads travel from your phone → the relay (optional) → your server. Klypp writes into a trio of folders: FileSaveLocation from the mobile drop, IncompletePath while files are being written, and CompletedPath when they are ready for the processor to move them into destinations such as Movies, TV, or Music. If you want to bypass the relay entirely, follow the Advanced: Direct Uploads steps and use a VPN so your phone talks straight to your server.

What Linux permissions helper should I run?

We publish a Linux Helper Script on the Downloads page that adjusts ownership/permissions for the klypp service user. Run that script once after installing to avoid “access denied” errors when the server scans your folders.

How should I configure FileSave, Incomplete, and Completed folders?

Always keep the three-folder flow: FileSaveLocation receives uploads from the mobile app, IncompletePath holds files while they are still being written, and CompletedPath is where our service picks up finished transfers to route them into Movies/TV/Music destinations. Writing directly into CompletedPath or skipping any of these steps can break processing.

Can I split media by 4K and kids content?

Yes. Add paired destinations for TV/TV4K and Movie/Movie4K, and optionally KidsTV/KidsTV4K and KidsMovie/KidsMovie4K to override standard folders for family-friendly titles. The Advanced movie setups and TV setups sections show practical examples.

How does TV drive priority work?

The app scans your listed TV drives in priority order (1→N). If it finds the show on a drive while scanning, it keeps writing new episodes there. If the show is absent from every listed drive, the new show lands on the last (N) drive. Add new drives with higher numbers so older shows keep writing to their current location. See TV: Multiple Drives & Priority for the exact behavior.

What are common setup mistakes to avoid?

Typical issues: missing the three-folder flow (FileSave → Incomplete → Completed), letting CompletedPath receive uploads directly, forgetting to run the Linux helper (permissions), removing a TV drive mid-series (it splits your collection), or forgetting to mark destinations like Kids or 4K. Always verify CompletedPath is writable by the klypp user.

What permissions does the mobile app need?

The app requests access to Files/Photos to select uploads, Local Network (iOS) to discover servers, and optional Notifications for transfer status. You can revoke any permission later in your device settings.

How do I bypass the relay and connect directly?

Install a VPN (Tailscale, ZeroTier, or WireGuard) on both the server and phone, then use the VPN IP when picking your server via Settings → Find Server in the mobile app. Direct uploads keep the relay out of the equation while still using HTTPS/TLS over the private network. Follow the direct upload guide for the full walkthrough.

How do I contact support or request data deletion?

Email support@klyppedit.com for help, and privacy@klyppedit.com to request account deletion or data export. Include your OS, KlyppServer version, and steps to reproduce so the team can respond faster.
